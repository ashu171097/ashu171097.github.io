UI VS UX

Two terms that are often used interchangeably, but actually mean very different things!

UX without UI

Like a brain without a body

UI without UX

Like a human without a brain

1

What Is a UI

“UI” stands for user interface. UI refers to the screens, buttons, toggles, icons, and other visual elements that you interact with when using a website, app, or other electronic device.

  • User interface: The means by which a person interacts with an application or hardware device
  • Typography: The style and appearance of written material; the art of making type legible, readable, and appealing
  • Breadcrumb: A way to show website users where they are in a website hierarchy (and how they got there)
  • Wireframe: A layout displaying functional elements of an interface
  • Easiest way to track time spent on tasks

2

What Is a UX

“UX” stands for user interface. UX refers to the entire interaction you have with a product, including how you feel about the interaction.

  • A/B testing - A method for comparing two versions of a product or service to evaluate which is more successful
  • Accessibility - The concept of whether a service or product can be used by people of all abilities, irrespective of their situation
  • End user - The person who will use a finished product or service once it has been purchased
  • Persona - A fictional representation of an ideal customer to help you understand their needs, goals, and behaviors
  • Information architecture - The structural design of information to make it more understandable
